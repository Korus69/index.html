Lesson 1

Chess Lesson for Beginner: Learning Piece Notations and Squares

Take a look at this chess board. This is where all the action happens, there are 64 squares, from the left to right notation as: a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h and from bottom to top, counting 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8. These notations will be very useful in next coming lessons because I am going to talk in chess notations and this helps to make learning process easier to the beginners. For example: The white king is on e1 and black king is on e8 square. Similarly, if I want to move a pawn on e2, to e4, this move would now be called as "e4". If I want to move my knight to f3, this move would be denoted as "Nf3".

Here are all the notations for every piece, remember that pawns do not have notation and whichever square pawn moves to, we are going to name that square only: Here's the list:

  • King - notation - K
  • Queen - notation - Q
  • Bishop - notation - B
  • Knight - notation - N
  • Rook - notation - R

Now for example, imagine a pawn is on e4 and another pawn is on d5, if someone captured this pawn, the notation would now be "exd5", where "x" or cross symbolizes something captured something, if Queen captured rook, "Qxr", etc.. so on. Even though the move has not happened in the board, you should be able to imagine it, this helps in your calculation of the move sequence. But more on that later at advanced level lessons.

Now that you have learned about notations of squares and pieces, let's move on to the next lesson, where I will show you how to begin the game with a beginner-friendly opening.
