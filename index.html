Lesson 2

Opening Concept and How to Learn Any Opening

I am sure you have mastered Lesson 1... So, the notation of castling is O-O, and for queenside it is O-O-O.

Here in the above picture, one opening has been played:

1. D4 D5
2. Bf4 e6
3. e3 Nf6
4. Nf3 c6
5. Bd3 Be7
6. O-O O-O

Now look at this position... This opening is called the Hyper-Accelerated London System.

Now you have watched this opening play out, try this on chess.com and lichess.org. Here are important goals of an opening:

  • Control the center (e4, e5, d4, d5)
  • Develop knights, bishops, and queen
  • Castle the king to safety
  • Achieve a solid middlegame position

Here, the bishop highlighted on d3 looks along the long diagonal, and later the queen can go to c2 to reinforce it.
